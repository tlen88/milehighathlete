Your Website Handbook

Everything you need to update milehighathlete.com yourself — written in plain English, no tech knowledge required.

Jump to: Welcome · Logging in · Dashboard tour · Editing text · Editing images · Programs & pricing · Previewing · Publishing · Undoing mistakes · SEO basics · Safety guidelines · Troubleshooting · Support

Welcome

Your website now has a private Site Manager — a login-protected portal where you can change what the website says and shows, without touching any code.

You can update: headlines, paragraphs, button text, your photo, program cards, reviews, the hyperbaric section, your Vagaro booking link, contact and social links, and how the site appears on Google.

You can't accidentally break: the design, colors, fonts, layout, animations, mobile behavior, or booking functionality. Those are locked. If a change would break the layout (like a headline that's far too long), the editor warns you before you can save it.

Logging in

  1. Go to milehighathlete.com/admin/ in any browser (phone works too — a laptop is comfier).
  2. Click “Log in to edit your website.”
  3. Enter your email and password. First visit? Open your invitation email, click Accept the invite, and choose a password.
  4. To log out, use the Log out button at the top right of the dashboard.

Forgot your password? On the login window, click “Forgot password?” and a reset link is emailed to you. If the email never arrives, check spam, then contact support (below).

Editing text

  1. Open a section card, e.g. Homepage Banner.
  2. Click into any field and type. Grey helper text under each field explains where it appears.
  3. Watch the preview pane on the right — it updates as you type.
  4. Click Save (top left). Your change is now a draft — not live yet.

Keep text short and punchy. Fields have character limits to protect the layout — if you see a red warning, trim the text and save again.

Editing images

  1. Open the section with the image (e.g. Homepage Banner → Your Photo).
  2. Click the image field, then “Choose an image” → Upload.
  3. Pick a file from your computer or phone.
  4. Fill in the Alt Text field — a one-line description of the photo (helps Google and visitors using screen readers).
  5. Save, then publish when ready.

Editing programs & pricing

  • Edit a program: open Training Programs, click the program's name to expand it, and edit any field.
  • Add a program: click “Add Programs” at the bottom of the list. Three programs looks best on desktop.
  • Remove a program: click the ✕ on that program — or better, turn “Show On Website” off to hide it without deleting it.
  • Reorder: drag programs up or down with the handle on the left.
  • Highlight one program: turn on “Highlight This Program” — it gets the featured style and badge (e.g. “Most Popular”). Use it on one program only.
  • Pricing: if you add prices to feature lines, write them exactly as you want them shown, e.g. “$150 per session”. Double-check numbers before publishing.

Previewing changes

While editing, the right-hand pane shows a live preview of your text using the real website fonts and colors. It's a close approximation — the final check is always the live website after you publish.

To go back to editing after previewing, just keep typing — or click Save and return to the dashboard.

Publishing changes

  1. After saving, your change sits in Draft. The live site hasn't changed yet.
  2. In the editor's top bar, click Status and set it to Ready.
  3. Click Publish → Publish now.
  4. The website rebuilds itself automatically. Give it a couple of minutes, then open milehighathlete.com and refresh (Cmd+Shift+R on Mac, Ctrl+Shift+R on Windows) to confirm your update is live.

If publishing fails: your changes are not lost — they stay saved as a draft. Wait a minute and try Publish again. Still failing? Contact support; nothing is broken on the live site.

Undoing mistakes

  • Before saving: just retype the old text, or leave the page without saving.
  • Saved but not published: open the draft and either fix it or delete the draft (Delete unpublished entry). The live site was never affected.
  • Already published: edit the section back to what it was and publish again — or contact support. Every published version is kept forever, so your developer can restore any previous version of the site exactly.

Safety guidelines

Safe to edit any time: headlines, paragraphs, button text, photos, alt text, programs, reviews, social links, SEO title/description.

Check with your developer first: the Website Address (canonical) field, the hyperbaric wellness disclaimer, and the scheduler height. They're editable, but wrong values can affect Google or the booking window.

Not editable here (on purpose): design, colors, fonts, layout, animations, and the booking system itself. If you want those changed, that's a developer job.
